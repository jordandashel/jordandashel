My Projects

At Nordstrom

AMP Color Extraction

I was tasked with taking on a high-profile machine learning collaboration with an in house data scientist. This project is now supporting a team of its own.

Our data scientist, Aaron, wrote an algorithm that could look at a picture of an item of clothing and determine the colors and distribution thereof in the item. It could then convert those RGB values into plain human language. For example, (200, 162, 200) -> "lilac". My job was to turn that into a production-ready web service. I wrote a Django wrapper around it and turned it into an EBS web app. Then I ran the service against a dozen million assets, storing the computed assets in a DynamoDb for quick lookup.

Migration to Kubernetes Plan

My team was heavily dependent on AWS's Elastic Beanstalk to support our network of approx. a dozen applications. My task was to first do a proof of concept of our applications in Docker Containers and then determine the pros and cons of a half dozen orchestration services. I was perfect for this task given my role on the team of tackling Ops issues, especially when stepping out of the java environment into the nuts and bolts of Linux VMs.

I built the team a tutorial course for using Docker and K8s, gave a ream of documentation and notes, and finally a suggestion for a path forward.

My Own Work

chesster

I enjoy chess and I am getting more into it. I was trying to follow along in the chess books I am reading but setting up a full chess board for example situations was such a hassle. I wanted a digital board I could set up and play with as I liked. All the chess apps I could find were only for playing chess, against people or the computer. I wanted one I could mess with just like a normal board. So I made this.

It is a strong indicator of how I program. It has unit tests (but no integration tests yet! -- coming soon) and is mostly following what I consider to be good coding practice. It's a good demo of code I could be writing for you.
